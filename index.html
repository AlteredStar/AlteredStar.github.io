There are 10 attributes that you may increase or decrease as the result of your decisions.


Health represents your physical body's condition. A steady nature that actively works to secure their well-being increases your health.
Weapons attuned to heatlh include gauntlets and quarter staffs.


Mana represents your capacity to handle magic. An inquisitive nature that pursues discovery of the unknown increases your mana.
Weapons attuned to mana include orbs, talismans, and jewels.


Strength represents your physical prowess. A bold nature that challenges problems head on increases your strength.
Weapons attuned to strength include heavy blades, axes, hammers, and polearms.


Vitality represents your ability to absorb hits. A resilient nature that remains unwavering increases your vitality.
Weapons attuned to vitality include shields and maces.


Dexterity represents your ability to control your body and handle tools. A resourceful nature that remains creative and precise increases your dexterity.
Weapons attuned to dexterity include daggers, claws, long bows, and spears.


Agility represents your ability to move swiftly. A flexible nature that remains free from all restraints increases your agility.
Weapons attuned to agility include shortswords, short bows, and chains.


Intelligence represents your potential to learn. A witty nature that picks up knowledge and skills quickly increases your intelligence
Weapons attuned to intelligence include scepters and wands.


Wisdom represents your judgement and foresight. A keen nature that grasps the complexities in life increases your wisdom.
Weapons attuned to wisdom include staves and tomes.


Spirit represents your resistance to mental and status effects. A virtuous nature that upholds strong morals increases your spirit.
Weapons attuned to spirit include crosses and scythes.


Charisma represents your charm as perceived by other entities. An outgoing nature that emanates kindness and makes decisions increases your charisma.
Weapons attuned to charisma include longswords, battle flags, and war horns.


Question 1: The First Crossroad, Beginnings

You are walking toward the city to prepare for the upcoming harsh winter and as the sun begins to fall, you come across a fork in the road.
As you continue to linger away from civilization the roads become increasingly dangerous.


The left path leads deeper into the forest where the existence of wolves is certain, but you will reach the city within 3 hours.

The right path leads to a clearing where a dim mansion can be seen in the distance, you estimate the distance to be about 1 hour away.

Behind you is a straight path leading back home, and you may rest at a town 1.5 hours away along the path.

Next to the crossroad is a small clearing fit for setting up camp.


Which path do you take?

Question 4: Artifact

Moreover, a message reading "This city shall fade at the behest of a greedy man" is inscribed in old characters on the wall behind the cages.

The second cage contains a golden goblet, inside you see a deep crimson liquid and the pungent smell of metal assaults your nose.

The third cage contains a pure black mantle; when you glance at it, the cloak's image tends to periodically dissipate and rematerialize in front of you.

Additionally, you may attempt to open one cage and unlock another by manipulating the mana enshrouding it; however, your escape is not guarenteed.

Question 5: Beginner Adventurer

You may challenge a hunting request with a party; although you will earn less money as the novice of the group, the chances of surviving and succeeding are much higher than challenging the request alone.

You may pick an escort request with a party; while the earnings are less than hunting quests, you will have a reliable method of reaching the Palace City where the rewards are more bountiful.


Which request do you choose?
