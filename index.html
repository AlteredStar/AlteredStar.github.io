Welcome to Character Creation!

There are 9 attributes that you may increase through your decisions.


Health represents your physical body's condition. A steadfast nature that actively seeks adventure increases your health.
Weapons attuned to heatlh are gauntlets and quarter staffs.


Mana represents your capacity to handle magic. An inquisitive nature that pursues discovery of the unknown increases your mana.
Weapons attuned to mana are orbs and talismans.


Strength represents your physical prowess. A bold nature that challenges problems with force increases your strength.
Weapons attuned to strength are greatswords, axes, and hammers.


Vitality represents your ability to absorb hits. A determined nature that remains unwavering regardless of the situation increase your vitality.
Weapons attuned to vitality are shields and maces.


Dexterity represents your ability to control your body and handle tools. A energetic nature that strives for growth.
Weapons attuned to dexterity are daggers, shortswords, bows, and pole-arms.


Intelligence represents your potential to learn. A witty nature that picks up knowledge and skills quickly increases your intelligence
Weapons attuned to intelligence are scepters and wands.


Wisdom represents your judgement and foresight. A keen nature that grasps that complexities of life increases your wisdom.
Weapons attuned to wisdom are staves and tomes.


Spirit represents your resistance to mental and status effects. A virtuous nature that upholds strong morals increases your spirit.
Weapons attuned to spirit are crosses and scythes.


Charisma represents your charm as perceived by other entities. An outgoing nature that emanates kindness inreases your charisma.
Weapons attuned to charisma are longswords, battle flags, and war horns.


Question 1: Crossroads

You are walking along a path in order to find essentials for the upcoming harsh winter season and as the sun begins to fall, you come across a fork in the road.


The left path leads deeper into the forest where the existence of wolves is certain, but you will reach a wealthy city within 3.5 hours.

The right path leads to a clearing where a dim mansion can be seen in the distance, you estimate the distance to be about 2.5 hour away.

Behind you is a straight path leading back home, and you may rest at a town 1.5 hours away along the path.

Next to the crossroad is a small clearing fit for setting up camp.


Which path do you take?











Question 2: Pouch of Gold

During a trip to the market, you find a pouch on the ground in an isolated alleyway containing a handful of gold coins, enough to let you live comfortably for half a year.


You may give the pouch to the nearby guards who have a reputation for being fair to the citizens of your town.

You may leave the pouch where it lies.

You may take the gold for yourself.

You may give the pouch to the local orphanage where you have aquainted yourself with its director.


What will you do with the pouch?











Question 3: Kingdom's Request

You have been transported into a fantasy world. You open your eyes in the King's audience chamber.
The Kingdom desperately pleas for your help to defeat the Demon Lord.


You may wholeheartedly agree to help the poor Kingdom, and your journey to defeat the Evil Demon Lord will begin.

You may agree to their request and decide your true alligance later, and regardless of whether or not you fight for yourself, the Kingdom, or against the Kingdom, your journey to defeat the Demon Lord will begin.

You may runaway once night falls after gathering information in the Palace, but you will become a wanted criminal in the Kingdom.

You may attempt to cajole the Princess in hopes of strengthening your actual influence in the Kingdom; however, success is not guaranteed, and you must stay allied with the Kingdom at least until your efforts have yielded a positive gain.


How will you react to the Kingdom's pleas?











Question 4: Artifact

You find yourself in a decorated room deep within the vestiges of an ancient city. The room contains 3 magical cages, each containing an item surrounded by a strong mana aura.
While exploring the ruins, you found a magic stone that appears to be a key, but the stone only has enough mana to unlock one cage.
Moreover, a message reading "This city shall fade at the behest of a greedy man" is inscribed in old characters on the wall behind the cages.


The first cage contains a sleek azure glove, and while standing next to the first cage you can clearly feel the temperature around it fluctuate.

The second cage contains a golden goblet, inside you see a deep crimson liquid and the pungent smell of metal assaults your nose.

The third cage contains a pure black mantle; when you glance at it, the cloak's image tends to periodically dissipate and rematerialize in front of you.

Additionally, you may attempt to open one cage and unlock another by manipulating the mana enshrouding it; however, your escape is not guarenteed.


Which cage do you open?











Question 5: Beginner Adventurer

You become an adventurer to earn money for your hometown, and after 1 week you have completed 3 gathering requests for the local guild.


You may take on another gathering request, but the only requests you can take have low yield to effort ratios.

You may challenge a solo hunting request to earn more money, but successfully completing a hunting request as a single beginner adventurer is not common and if you cannot complete the request in 3 days the guild will penalize you.

You may challenge a hunting request with a party; although you will earn less money as the novice of the group, the chances of surviving and succeeding are much higher than challenging the request alone.

You may pick an escort request with a party; while the earnings are less than hunting quests, you will have a reliable method of reaching the Palace City where the rewards are more bountiful.


Which request do you choose?











Question 6: Bandit Encounter

While on an escort request you encounter a group of 7 bandits blocking the road.
Your party consists of yourself, one warrior, one archer, and the merchant.


You may cooperate with your party to route the bandits.

You may take it upon yourself to step up and negotiate with the bandits before any fighting can occur.

You may flee the scene, but the bandits will send 1 bandit to chase after you.

You may attempt to surrender, and if your life is ever at risk you may use whatever means available to escape.


How will you survive the bandit encounter?
